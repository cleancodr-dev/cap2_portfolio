Capstone 2 Project.

This is my version of the Capstone 2 Project from Angela Yu's Fullstack Bootcamp.The code verison of my coding Draft. Unfortunaly this exercise should be done with just HTML and CSS, and this leaded into many ideas that ended up in my virtual trashcan. While searching for the best design, i already made it over and over again on my Ipad. So here we are: A virtual sketchbook styled website, with draftlike design elements which can be seen when your viewport is bigger than 1024pixel. Like its best practice, i made a mobile-first site and consequently choose just one breakpoint for the design elements that give it this draft feeling, or how i like to call it: Draft-ception.

Now i want to I want to tell you a bit, what i did here:
Firt of all, i build up a grid system with one row and two columns. And i repeated this, for every section inside my code. The Idea is, that i break down my site into the semantic Html elements, too. I tried to make visible, what normaly is hidden inside your browser. And as little bonus, i did not just wanted to write it down like a list, i wanted to add it dynamicly to the specific sections. As you can see, the text you are reading in this moment is wrapped by the <main>Tag. (Just in the Desktop version. SORRY!) This way, i created a visual representation of the underlying structure of my HTML, not just for fun, but as a conceptual experiment. Each section acts like a page of my digital sketchbook, where markup and layout meet design thinking.
On smaller screens, I kept everything clean and minimal, the semantic part is hidden, so users can focus on the actual content. But once the viewport gets wider, the draft elements appear. They don't just add a nice visual twist, but also tell a story. A story about how a developer structures ideas, tests concepts, and evolves design through code, even without JavaScript.

This project taught me a lot about limitations of HTML/CSS and creativity. Sometimes, working with restrictions helps you to discover smarter solutions. What you see here is not the perfect final product, it's a snapshot of the creative process itself. And honestly,...
that’s exactly what i wanted to capture.
